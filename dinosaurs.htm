The different species

Animantarx

The Animantarx is the first dinosaur to be discovered by technology alone. In 1999, a sensitive Geiger counter (instrument used for detecting and measuring radiation) was used and the fossils of the species were discovered, since dinosaur fossils are slightly radioactive. Not a lot is known about this dinosaur since it is the only specimen that has been discovered and not its full skeleton has been found yet. 

	
Length: uncertain due to lack of bones
	Diet: herbivorous
	Type: nodosaurus
	Living era: Early Cretaceous
	Found in: USA
	Type species: A.‭ ‬ramaljonesi‭

Ankylosaurus

The Ankylosaurus caught my attention because it closely resembles the final boss of Super Mario 64, Bowser. This species was one of the largest armored dinosaurs that have been discovered, it had a heavily armored skull and a large tail club (bony mass at the end of the tail). It is suspected all this armor was to protect itself, since it did not eat other dinosaurs.

	
Length: 7 meters
	Diet: herbivorous
	Type: Thyreophora
	Living era: Late Cretaceous
	Found in: Canada, USA
	Type species: A. magniventris‭

Carnotaurus

Carnotaurus, as the name states, was a carnivorous theropod that roamed the lands of what is now modern-day Argentina. The meaning of its name is “meat-eating bull” because of its horns. As of today, the Carnotaurus is the only theropod to possess horns. The horns were more likely used as a tool to fight for females of the same species.

	
Length: 7.6 meters
	Diet: Carnivorous
	Type: Large Theropod
	Living era: Late Cretaceous
	Found in: Argentina
	Type species: Sastrei‭

Drinker

The Drinker did not drink… It got its name from its discoverer, Edward Drinker Cope. It was an Ornithopod and its length was only 2 meters! This species is now considered a synonym of the Nanosaurus along with the Othnielia, and Othnielosaurus.

	
Length: 2 meters
	Diet: Herbivorous
	Type: Ornithopod
	Living era: Late Jurassic to Early Cretaceous
	Found in: Canada, USA
	Type species: Drinker Nisti‭

Dynamoterror

Not only its name sounds menacing, it simply was. Averaging at a length of 9 meters, this carnivorous could be proof of how the tyrannosaurids evolved their notoriously large body size and established themselves as the top predators of the day. It is now considered the “cousin” of the T-Rex.

	
Length: 9 meters
	Diet: Carnivorous
	Type: Tyrannosauroid theropod
	Living era: Late Cretaceous
	Found in: USA
	Type species: Dynamoterror Dynastes

Futalognkosaurus

The futalognkosaurus name was not the only thing that was long about this species, but also its structure. Even though it is not really known how long it was because, except for a single vertebra, the dinosaur’s tail is entirely missing, specialists have estimated the futalognkosaurus to be between 32-34 meters long.

	
Length: 32-34 meters
	Diet: Herbivorous
	Type: Sauropod
	Living era: Late Cretaceous
	Found in: Argentina
	Type species: Futalognkosaurus Dukei‭

Giganotosaurus

For the longest time, it was believed the T-Rex was the “King of dinosaurs”. However, the Giganotosaurus came and stole that spot! As the name suggests, it was giant, its length was around 12.5 m. and it was carnivorous. It was so powerful that it is speculated it hunted the Argentinosaurus species (they were about 40 m. in length). Giganotosaurus had 3 fingers on its hands, not 2 like T.rex.

	
Length: 12.5 meters
	Diet: Carnivorous
	Type: Large theropod
	Living era: Early Cretaceous
	Found in: Argentina
	Type species: Giganotosaurus Carolinii

Hippodraco

Don’t let the name of this dinosaur fool you, it does not look like a hippopotamus. It resembles a more classic dinosaur figurine that can be found in any toy shop. The hippodraco was a herbivore that lived during the Early Cretaceous and was found in the United States of America.

	
Length: 4.5 meters
	Diet: Herbivorous
	Type: Ornithopod
	Living era: Early Cretaceous
	Found in: USA
	Type species: Tenontosaurus‭

Laquintasaura

This little guy was the first for many things. It was the first dinosaur to be found in the north side of South America, more specifically in what is now known as the country of Venezuela. It is also the first ornithischian to be found that lived in herds. And also, even though it was herbivorous, the curve of its teeth suggests that it might have eaten insects and small prey, however, this has not been truly confirmed.

	
Length: 1 meter
	Diet: Herbivorous (curve of some of its teeth 
     suggest it might have also feasted on insects 
      and small prey) 
	Type: Ornithischian
	Living era: End of Triassic period
	Found in: Venezuela
	Type species: Laquintasaura Venezuelae

Sarahsaurus

The Sarahsaurus was a giant four-legged dinosaur that lived during the late Triassic to the early Jurassic era. Even though it had a lot in common with earlier sauropods, it was also starting to show features in how this clade (a group of reptiles, often very large) would evolve into massive animals, these features were: an increase in body size and lengthening of the neck vertebrae.

	
Length: 4 meters
	Diet: Herbivorous
	Type: Sauropod
	Living era: Late Triassic and Early Jurassic 
	Found in: USA
	Type species:  Sarahsaurus Aurifontanalis

Tachiraptor

The Tachiraptor is a small carnivorous that averages in the length of 1.5 m. Its name means “The Thief of Tachira” since it was found in what is now the state of Tachira, Venezuela. They were likely generalist predators, preying on smaller vertebrates like other dinosaurs or lizards.

	
Length: 1.5 meters
	Diet: Carnivorous 
	Type: small Theropod
	Living era: Early Jurassic 
	Found in: Venezuela
	Type species: Tachiraptor admirabilis

Triceratops

The triceratops can be considered one of the most famous species of dinosaurs out there, and not for nothing, it had a parrot-like beak and large frill that could reach nearly 1 meter! Triceratops skull is one of the largest and most striking of any land animal. It was herbivorous, and it mainly ate tough palm fronds. The triceratops lived during the Late Creatceous period, found in the United States.

	
Length: 9 meters
	Diet: Herbivorous
	Type: Ceratopsian
	Living era: Late Cretaceous 
	Found in: USA
	Type species:  Horridus

Tyrannosaurus

The Tyrannosaurus might be one of (if not the most) famous species of dinosaurs out there. It has appeared in multiple Hollywood movies, like the Jurassic Park franchise. Even a person that barely knows about dinosaurs will know about this one! Tyrannosaurus Rex means “Tyrant Lizard King” and it makes it justice, this species was one of the biggest carnivores in America.

	
Length: 12 meters
	Diet: Carnivorous 
	Type: Large Theropod
	Living era: Late Cretaceous 
	Found in: Canada, USA
	Type species: Rex

Utahraptor

The Utahraptor is a carnivorous dinosaur that lived during the Early Cretaceous period. It was found in the United States of America, in the state of Utah. Did you know that in Utah, more than 70 individual dinosaurs have been found?

	
Length: 6 meters
	Diet: Carnivorous
	Type: Large Theropod
	Living era: Early Cretaceous 
	Found in: USA
	Type species: Ostrommaysorum

Dracorex

The Dracorex is no common dinosaur, it was the first flat-headed Pachycephalosaurs found in North America. This dino is extra special for Harry Potter fans, its scientific name is the Dracorex Hogwatsia, it gained this title because when the fossils were found and it was reconstructed it looked like a magical beast that deserved to be in the Harry Potter series.
