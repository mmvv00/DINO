Important Definitions

Ankylosauria: From the suborder ornisthischian. Characterized for having a long low-lying thickset body covered dorsally with bony plates.

Carnivore: Diet consists of other animals.

Ceratopsia: From the suborder ornisthischian. Characterized for having horns, a sharp horny beak, and a bony frill projecting backward from the skull.

Clade: Natural group of dinosaurs that share common ancestry.

Herbivore: Diet consists of only plants.

Omnivore: Diet consists of both plants and animals.

Ornithischian: Herbivorous. The pubis is of the pelvis rotated backward to a position parallel and close to the ischium.

Ornithopod: Bipedal ornithischian dinosaur with digitigrade walking limbs usually having only three functional toes.

Saurischia: Hervibovorus or carnivorous. The pubis of the pelvis is typically pointed downward and forward.

Sauropod: Clade of saurischia dinosaurs. They had very long necks, long tails, small heads, and four thick, pillar-like legs. They were also the biggest animals to ever roam earth.

Stegosauria: A suborder of Ornithischia. Characterized for developing a strong dorsal bony armor.

Theropod: Clade of saurischia dinosaurs. Their characteristics were being carnivores, having a big head, powerful legs, walked on two legs, weren't very fast, and have hollow bones.

Type species: The specifical species from which the genus is named, these are considered to be permanently taxonomically associated.
